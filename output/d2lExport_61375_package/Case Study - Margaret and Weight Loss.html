Case Study: Margaret and Weight Loss

Estimated time to complete: 90 minutes.

Margaret is 37 years-old. She is 5'3" and weighs 160 pounds. Her waist circumference is 38 inches and her percent body fat is 36%. She has been trying to lose weight off and on for a number of years. She has tried a variety of trendy diets, each gave her some temporary success, but she always seems to gain the weight back and sometimes even more.

Margaret doesn’t enjoy exercise and hopes she can achieve her objective without having to do any physical activity. She has heard that a number of her friends are trying a low carbohydrate approach to weight loss and wonders if this might be the key to her success. She also wonders if gastric surgery may be the answer.

Activity

Obesity is a growing concern worldwide. Managing weight requires maintaining a balance between energy intake and energy expenditure. In this activity you will practice evaluating Margaret's BMI and percent body fat and provide her with suggestions for successful weight loss.

  1. Review the information on body weight and health and managing body weight in your textbook (page 310-314, 327-337).
  2. Read the guidelines for bariatric surgery discussed by the Mayo Clinic staff and watch the "Frontline Fat" video.
