L05 Concluding Cases

Due by the End of Week, see Calendar

Instructions

Use the Concluding Cases to connect the disease information you studied to additional real-world situations.

Step 01

Follow the case study process to critically evaluate each situation described below. You can complete this activity on your own before reviewing with your study partner or groups, or you may complete this activity for the first time with your study partner or group.

[]

Case Study A

Carl, a 25 yo M, presents himself to your clinic complaining of painful lumps on his neck. He tells you yesterday one of these lumps burst and a large amount of pus was discharged. As you examine these “lumps” you notice that the area around them has started to become swollen and red.

Case Study B

Charlotte, a 10 yo F, is brought to your clinic by her mother. Charlotte tells you that her head has been extremely itchy the past few days. You examine Charlotte’s head and find nits on the shafts of her hair. You also find eggs that the nits have planted in her hair.

Case Study C

Tim, a 16 yo M, presents himself to your clinic and tells you he has been experiencing an intense itching sensation on his feet. Tim also says that he experiences a burning sensation around his toes. He tells you he is a wrestler for his high school. You ask him if he ever walks around barefoot in the locker room, and he replies that he does.
[]

Case Study D

Nancy, a 30 yo F, presents herself to your clinic and tells you she has been experiencing intense itching in her pubic area. She also tells you she has noticed an abnormal vaginal discharge.

Case Study E

Shirley, a 28 yo F, presents herself to your clinic and tells you she has had intense itching sensations between her fingers, on her elbows, and on her waist. You examine these areas and find thread-like lesions. She tells you the itching intensifies at night, and scratching it only makes it worse.

Case Study F

Damien, a 16 yo M, presents himself to your clinic and tells you he has had these “zits” on his face near his mouth and nose that have erupted and left a thick yellow crust on his skin. You examine these “zits” and find that they are pustules. You ask him if he keeps up with his personal hygiene, and he says that he tries to, but he wrestles and the mats aren’t always clean.
[]

Case Study G

Louise is a 19 yo F who presents herself to your clinic with “bumps” on her fingers. She tells you she noticed them a few days ago and they are extremely tender and itchy. She tells you that her boyfriend had something similar on his hands, and a few days after holding hands and walking around campus is when she noticed them.

Case Study H

Nancy is a 30 yo F African American who presents herself to your clinic and says she has “white spots” on her hands and face. You conduct a physical examination and find that, like she said, there are white patches on her hands and face.

Case Study I

Keith is a 27 yo M who presents himself to your clinic and tells you his hands have turned blue. You look at his hands and find that his fingers have actually turned blue. You ask him when he noticed this and he said when he got back from his trip to Hawaii back to the cold temperatures of Rexburg is when he noticed it. He also tells you he has noticed that his fingers are unusually stiff and swollen.
[]

Case Study J

Danny, a 34 yo M, presents himself to your clinic and tells you he has noticed yellow “flakes” in his hair as well as in his beard. He tells you he has noticed these “flakes” on his shirt at the end of the day.

Case Study K

Sandra, a 23 yo F, presents herself to your clinic and tells you she has a rash that itches and burns. You ask where the rash is and she tells you it’s on her arms and legs. You ask when she noticed it and she tells you her arms and legs turned red after she went hiking with her husband. You ask if she noticed any poison ivy in the area she hiked in and she said her husband thought he saw some, but they weren’t sure.

Case Study L

Elmer, a 25 yo M, presents himself to your clinic and tells you he has noticed some sort of rash on his face, neck, and torso, and it is extremely itchy. You ask him if he is allergic to anything and he tells you he isn’t sure, but he did start some new medication recently. You examine his rash, and notice that this rash is severe lesions along with leathery vesicles on his face, neck, torso, as well as the bends on his knees.
[]

Case Study M

Martha is a 19 yo F who presents herself to your clinic and tells you she has just recently received a mission call to South America and she’s been trying to get through her last semester before she leaves. She’s taking 18 credits and has been extremely stressed. She shows you two small lesions on her top lip. You ask her if she has experience this before and she tells you she hasn’t, but her boyfriend has had something similar before.

Case Study N

Stanley, a 57 yo M, presents himself to your clinic and tells you he has been experiencing severe pain in his left shoulder recently. He tells you the area is extremely itchy. You look at his shoulder and find several large, raised vesicles along the left side of his upper body. You ask him if he’s ever had chicken pox, and he tells you that he had them when he was 8 years old.

Case Study O

Cathy, a 60 yo F, presents herself to your clinic and tells you she found an unusual marking on her left forearm. You examine this “marking” and find that it is firm red nodule on her arm that appears to have scales on it. You ask her if she wears sunscreen and she tells you she does now, but she didn’t for most of her life and she grew up working on the family farm.
