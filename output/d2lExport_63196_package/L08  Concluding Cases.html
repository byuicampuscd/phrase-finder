Instructions

Use the Concluding Cases to connect the disease information you studied to additional real-world situations.

Step 01

Follow the case study process to critically evaluate each situation described below. You can complete this activity on your own before reviewing with your study partner or groups, or you may complete this activity for the first time with your study partner or group.

[]

Case Study A

Tim, a 46 yo African American M, presents himself to your clinic and tells you that he has been getting up to go to the bathroom 5 or 6 times every night. He also experiences lightheadedness and a ringing sound in his ears. From Tim’s appearance, you can tell that he is overweight. You ask him about his diet and he tells you he eats at McDonald’s every day because he’s not married and doesn’t like cooking. On physical exam, Tim’s blood pressure is 164/102.

Case Study B

Betty, a 37 yo F, presents herself to your clinic and tells you she has been having headaches and dizzy spells lately. At night she’ll have chest pain and her muscles will cramp up during the day. You ask her how much she exercises and she tells you never because it causes her “smoker’s cough” to become unbearable.

Case Study C

Louise, a 64 yo F, presents herself to your clinic and tells you she is feeling a tightness in her chest. She also says that she feels like she has a stomach ache and there is a pain in the middle of her back that won’t go away.
[]

Case Study D

Stan, a 60 yo M, is brought to your clinic by his wife. She tells you that Stan has been experiencing chest pain and shortness of breath. He also seems to be losing his memory because he is sometimes confused and asks her where he is or forgets to finish something he had been doing.

Case Study E

Ronda, a 52 yo F, is brought to your clinic by her husband. He tells you they were out gardening when Ronda started vomiting and could not catch her breath. She says that she felt a pain in her chest, but she thought that it was because she couldn’t catch her breath.

Case Study F

Brent, a 59 yo M, presents himself to your clinic and tells you he has noticed his veins in his neck have started bulging and his skin is starting to turn blue. You order an ultrasound to be done and find that his liver is enlarged.
[]

Case Study G

Paula, a 47 yo F, presents herself to your clinic with her husband. He tells you she has been complaining of chest pain for the past few days and has felt lightheaded. Today, she started experiencing shortness of breath and told him that it felt like her heart was going to pound out of her chest.

Case Study H

Debbie, a 29 yo F, presents herself to your clinic and tells you she has had a dull aching pain in her right leg. You examine her leg and find that it is red and tender to touch. She tells you she had knee surgery and so she hasn’t moved that leg much in a few months.

Case Study I

Tracey, a 46 yo F, presents herself to your clinic and tells you she has had a dull aching pain in her left leg. She tells you she works at Walmart and she is exhausted after standing behind the counter during her shift. You examine her leg and find bulging veins on the back of her leg.
[]

Case Study J

Terry, a 53 yo M, presents himself to your clinic and tells you he has noticed lumps in his neck and armpits. He says that every time he bumps something, he instantly bruises. At night he will wake up drenched in sweat and has had a fever for the past few weeks.

Case Study K

Henry, a 33 yo M, presents to your clinic and tells you his leg has been swelling up and he can’t get it to stop. He tells you it doesn’t hurt, but he can’t get anything but shorts on because his leg is so big.

Case Study L

Wanda, a 48 yo F, presents herself to your clinic and tells you she has noticed lumps in her neck, armpits, and groin area. She has lost about 15 pounds in the past month and she is always exhausted.
