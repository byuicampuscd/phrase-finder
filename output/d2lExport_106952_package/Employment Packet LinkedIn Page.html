PART 1: COVER LETTER

Instructions

Before you begin this assignment, read/view all items in the "Unit 1 Presentation & Materials" page. For this assignment, you will be evaluated on your adherence to principles taught in the materials provided there.

This assignment is the first of three documents that you will produce as part of an employment packet. You will also create a résumé and a LinkedIn page. (See the Unit 1 Overview.)

The purpose of this exercise is to practice writing for an audience to showcase your experiences and qualifications for a position.

A cover letter should entice the reader to interview you. As such, this is a persuasive document where you “sell yourself.” Most successful cover letters include an opening paragraph where the writer states the position he or she is applying for and perhaps how the candidate heard about the job. This paragraph may also briefly identify why you are a strong candidate.

The next one or two paragraphs should link your education and experience to the specific qualifications for the position. Conclude with a paragraph that ties the letter together and explains how the reader can contact you.

Download the attached Word document and write your letter in the document. It contains a grading rubric table that your instructor will use later to evaluate your work. Do not edit or delete the table. Your document should be patterned after this format example.

Part PART 2: RÉSUMÉ

Instructions

Before you begin this assignment, read/view all items in them "Unit 1 Presentation & Materials" page. For this assignment, you will be evaluated on your adherence to principles taught in the materials provided there.

The purpose of this exercise is to practice writing a summary of your experiences and qualifications for a specific position. You should choose a position that you can imagine yourself applying for, such as an internship or job upon graduation. Use your current experience and knowledge to showcase how you are a good fit for the job. In other words, do not invent skills or experiences you do not already have.

Arrange your résumé either chronologically or functionally. Include education information (including key courses and honors, if any), experience, and activities/recognitions/interests. Your résumé should be no longer than one page and must fit the requirements of your field.

Your résumé will be evaluated on design, formatting, style, language, and conciseness.

PART 3: LINKEDIN PAGE

Instructions

Before you begin this assignment, read/view all items in the "Unit 1 Presentation & Materials" page. For this assignment, you will be evaluated on your adherence to principles taught in the materials provided there.

The job search and hiring process has changed dramatically during the last five years. Employers and professionals in almost every industry are now utilizing online sites such as Facebook, LinkedIn, and others to network and share job opportunities. Your success in the competitive job market may depend in part on your ability to use social media.

Create a free profile on LinkedIn and create or update the following sections: Experience, Courses, Skills & Expertise, Volunteer Experience, and Education. Join three professional “groups” with 100+ members in your field.

Access this Prezi presentation, developed by the BYU–Idaho alumni office, for information about how to create a LinkedIn profile. 

Join the BYU–Idaho Mentors group. Invite at least 30 powerful contacts (people you know personally). This should include people in our class. Add your instructor as a contact so he/she can evaluate your LinkedIn presence.

Your LinkedIn profile will be evaluated on how well you follow the directions and specifications provided in the assignment description.

After you have created your LinkedIn profile and connected with your teacher and others, submit the URL for your LinkedIn profile. The URL can be found under your profile picture. It will be an address like www.linkedin.com/in/yourname.
