L12 Concluding Cases

Due by End of Week, see Calendar

Estimated Time: 2 hours

Instructions

Use the Concluding Cases to connect the disease information you studied to additional real-world situations.

Step 01

Follow the case study process to critically evaluate each situation described below. You can complete this activity on your own before reviewing with your study partner or groups, or you may complete this activity for the first time with your study partner or group.

[]

Case Study A

The Kristofs, a couple in their late thirties, having been trying unsuccessfully to conceive a child for 15 months. They have come to you to determine if this is normal or if they should be concerned.

Case Study B

Nathan, a 21 yo M, presents himself to your clinic with symptoms of painful and frequent urination and a greenish discharge from his penis.

Case Study C

Stephanie, a 34 yo F, has reoccurring pain and itching on her external genitalia. During examination you notice small, red ulcerations and vesicles on the labia. Stephanie says the sores are very uncomfortable.
[]

Case Study D

Brandon, a 19 yo sexually active M, is in the clinic today complaining of dark growths around the edge of the glands of his penis. He noticed them a few weeks ago, but they have become more numerous.

Case Study E

Jayme, a 26 yo F, is in for her routine annual physical and pap smear. During your examination, you notice a number of red ulcerations or chancres on the outer labia. You ask if the areas are painful. Jayme says they do not hurt at all and she never even noticed them.

Case Study F

Francine, a 38 yo F, presents to your clinic with complaint of a “foamy” greenish/yellow discharge. Her genitalia has been burning and she now has noticed the discharge. She states that it has a bad odor.
[]

Case Study G

James, a 53 yo M, has had difficulty emptying his bladder. He has to get up from bed two or three times during the night to go to the restroom. This has been getting progressively worse over the past year.

Case Study H

Frank, a 61 yo M, is an active triathlete. He has noticed pain in the lower part of his back. He wonders if it is because of his bicycle riding. He also says that he has to urinate more regularly than before, but thought that was because of age.

Case Study I

Steve, a 26 yo M, walks or rather “waddles” into your clinic. He has a slight fever. His scrotum and groin area are very tender and sore. He has been previously treated for STIs on two occasions.
[]

Case Study J

During a community cancer screening event, Zach, a 23 yo M, was referred to you. He has a medium-sized painless mass near his left testicle.

Case Study K

Sydney, a 19 yo F, is a college athlete. She has come to the clinic because she “quit having my period about 3 months ago.”

Case Study L

Caitlyn, a 28 yo F, is experiencing irritability and pain, cramping, and spasms in the lower abdomen. This usually occurs the day before she starts her period. Caitlyn has heavy periods that last 7–8 days. She reports that she smokes about 15 cigarettes a day.
[]

Case Study M

Gloria, a 29 yo F, is having an annual check up at the clinic. She reports pain in her lower back and “between her legs” and sometimes painful urination. She also has been trying to get pregnant for about 8 months, but is having a difficult time. During her period, she had a lot of pain just below her belly button.

Case Study N

Anna, a 39 yo F, is having a routine pelvic examination. You notice a mass. Anna uses a birth control pill, has been noticing more pressure in her pelvis, and sometimes urine leaks out when she sneezes. Her periods are very heavy.

Case Study O

Carla, a 49 yo F, has arrived at your clinic. She is quite nervous. She says, “Please tell me I am not pregnant. Not at 49 years old.” Carla reports that her periods have been much lighter over the past 6 months, but two months ago, they just stopped. After further questioning she also reports night sweats and occasionally a feeling like someone is “messing around” with the thermometer at work.
[]

Case Study P

Helena is a 65 yo F who reports feeling bloated over the last two months and feels full after eating only a small meal. She has also noticed that her clothes are feeling tighter, but she is not gaining much weight. On examination, you palpate a lower abdominal mass on her right side. A CT shows a mass on her ovary.

Case Study Q

Crystal, a 41 yo F, has noticed a number of medium sized lumps in her left breast over the past six months or so. Her breasts are more tender and painful prior to her menstrual cycle. The lumps are on the upper portion of the breast. An ultrasound identifies that the masses are hard and not fluid filled.

Case Study R

Wynnona, a 40 yo F, has had a routine mammogram. Her results showed an abnormal mass on her right breast. During examination there is a noticeable lump and dimpling of the skin in the area of the mass. You order a biopsy of the mass. The results will be back to you tomorrow.
[]

Case Study S

Brenda, a 23 yo F, has come back to your clinic today complaining of cramping and bleeding from the vagina. You confirmed that she was pregnant 6 weeks ago. As of today, she is 14 weeks pregnant.

Case Study T

Sally, a 25 yo F, reports to the emergency room with extensive abdominal pain. Sally and her husband have been trying to have a baby. She is three weeks late for her period. She has no fever and extreme pain when palpating the lower middle and right side of her abdomen.

Case Study U

Belinda is a 22 yo F who is expecting her first baby. During her 32-week checkup, she has high levels of protein in her urine sample, she has gained 5 pounds since her last appointment two weeks ago, is complaining of a slight headache, and her blood pressure is 184/108.
[]

Case Study V

Brookie, a 32 yo F, is expecting her fourth child. She is 36 weeks pregnant and has started to notice a bright red, bloody discharge. She has no other symptoms. An ultrasound shows that the placenta sits low in the uterus.

Case Study W

Isabella, a single 21 yo F, is expecting twin babies. She has been warned on multiple occasions to avoid the use of drugs, alcohol, and tobacco. However, she continues to abuse drugs occasionally. Today she has shown up at the clinic with a significant amount of bloody discharge. Her abdomen is very rigid. She is 23 weeks along in her pregnancy.
