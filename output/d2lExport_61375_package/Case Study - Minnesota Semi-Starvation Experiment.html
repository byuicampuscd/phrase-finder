Case Study: Minnesota Semi-Starvation Experiment

Estimated time to complete: 90 minutes.

In the United States and many other developed countries, excessive weight gain is a major health concern. It is common for people to try to lower their weight by going on very low calorie diets to get quick results. Have you ever wondered what happens to the body, both physically and psychologically, when it is deprived of food for an extended time?

During the last year of World War II, Ancel Keys conducted a study in human starvation to determine how to best feed people after the war. Thirty-six conscientious objectors to war participated in the study. One of the participants, Jim Greene, gave a 22-minute interview of what happened during the study.
