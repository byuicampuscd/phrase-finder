L11: AE - Application Exercise: Challenging the System

Due End of Week 

In this assignment, you have the opportunity to "challenge the system" in some way.

In the previous lesson you were assigned to watch some clips from the movie The Sound of Music. In the Von Trapp Family System, challenging the rules is unacceptable.  Maria gives us a good example of "challenging the system" as she confronts the father's rigid rules in the [second clip] . We also see her challenging the system in the [first clip when she asked]  the Captain what is his whistle signal. 

Salvador Minuchin, the noted Family Systems therapist, wrote that challenging the system "suggests a search for new patterns … The old order must be undermined, to allow for the formation of the new." (Minuchin, 1981, Family Therapy Techniques, p. 67). 

Instructions

Step 1

Find some way to challenge an existing system (pattern) in your family or among your friends. Observe what happens. 

Here are some examples:

  • Let's say my family has a pattern of not showing emotion or saying "I love you". I could go home and give my dad a big hug and say "I love you." Then I could do the same to my other family members and see how the family responds. Do they try and shut it down with negative feedback loops?
  • Another example could be challenging the system by sitting in someone's place at the dinner table OR
  • kindly confronting a roommate who is consistently breaking curfew (the system being that usually everyone  no one says anything).

Step 2

Write a paper between 100 - 350 words addressing these questions. Each question should be a heading in your paper.

  • What was the "system" (pattern) before you challenged it?
  • What did you do?
  • How did the system respond?
  • Do you feel the system needs to be challenged?
