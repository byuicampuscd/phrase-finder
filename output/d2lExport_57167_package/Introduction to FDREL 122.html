Introduction to FDREL 122

Welcome to Book of Mormon 2: Alma 30 - Moroni

Below are explanations of the activities for each week. You will find a similar page at the beginning of every lesson telling you what activities to complete, when they are due, and an approximation of the time it will take to complete each one-although you may find you need a little more (or less) time on some of them. The Learn activities are generally due first; they will prepare you for the rest of the lesson's activities and assessments. Plan ahead so that you can finish everything before it is due. You can navigate to the different activities either by clicking on the activity in the left hand menu or using the links provided on the Overview pages. In either case, make sure you understand what is expected each week, and when, so that you can get the most from the course.

As you work on assignments, be proactive by thinking about how your efforts can help you to learn, do, and become a better disciple of Jesus Christ. Below is a list of Learn, Do and Become activities you will participate in throughout the course, along with a description of the activity.

]

Learn 

  • Questions and Conversations - This is the place to ask questions about your assignments and the course.
  • Assigned Readings - Scripture block assignments you will read to learn about and study specific doctrine and principles. 
  • Supplemental Readings - Prophetic teachings relevant to this week's scripture block. 
  • Directed Study -  An activity to help you focus on a significant theme or principle within this week's scripture block.

Do

  • Digging Deeper - Watching videos to help you gain a deeper insight into the doctrines and principles discussed within the reading block. 
  • Doctrinal Discussion Board-  A place to share your insights from the scripture block with you classmates and instructor, and learn what insights your classmates are having. Try to share something that could be uplifting or encouraging for your classmates to better understand. 
  • Share it! - An activity where you are asked to share your scriptural insights with friends or family, and reporting how you did so. 

Become

  • Personal Study Reports - Report on your completion of this the readings and other learning activities each week. 
  • Course Projects - Projects where you will have the chance to ponder the doctrine and principles you've learned.
