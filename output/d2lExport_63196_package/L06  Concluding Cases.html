L07 Concluding Cases

Due by End of Week, see Calendar

Estimated Time: 2 hours

Instructions

Use the Concluding Cases to connect the disease information you studied to additional real-world situations.

Step 01

Follow the case study process to critically evaluate each situation described below. You can complete this activity on your own before reviewing with your study partner or groups, or you may complete this activity for the first time with your study partner or group.

[]

Case Study A

Claire, a 25 yo F, presents herself to your clinic. She tells you she has been experiencing extreme pain in the right frontal region of her head. She says "it feels like someone is pounding my head with a hammer." You ask how long this has been occurring and she replies it's been happening off and on for the past few weeks.

Case Study B

Anna, a 25 yo F, presents herself to your clinic and tells you she has been experiencing severe headaches and started throwing up. She said she was at work the other day and she had a seizure, which has never happened before. She also tells you she has had a stiff neck for some reason. You ask her if she has had any illnesses lately and she tells you she just got over pneumonia.

Case Study C

Stan, a 50 yo M, presents himself to your clinic and tells you that his feet feel "tingly." He says that his feet have become very sensitive to touch, which is unusual because he's never had this problem before. You ask about his health history and he tells you he has had diabetes for about 10 years.
[]

Case Study D

Nancy, a 52 yo F, presents herself to your clinic and tells you that she woke up this morning and the right side of her face was drooping. She can't keep the right side of her mouth closed and can't stop drooling. She also says that she can't close her eye.

Case Study E

Keith, a 72 yo M, is brought to your clinic by his wife. She tells you he was sitting in his chair when all of a sudden he went limp, like he fell asleep. When she got him to wake up, he wasn’t able to talk right and slurred his words. He seemed to be extremely confused, and was having difficulties walking straight.

Case Study F

Lucille, a 78 yo F, is brought to your clinic by her grandson. He tells you that he went to visit Lucille, and when he arrived he found her slumped over in her chair. He woke her up and noticed that her right arm was limp by her side. He helped her get up to use the restroom, but when she tried to walk she started to stagger and almost fell. Lucille was taken to the hospital where her symptoms completely resolved in 24 hours.
[]

Case Study G

Jerry, a 25 yo M, presents himself to your clinic and tells you he has been experiencing seizures. You ask him if any event may have caused this, and he tells you his horse bucked him off a few weeks ago and he hit his head on a log.

Case Study H

Janice, a 80 yo F, is brought to your clinic by her daughter. Her daughter tells you that her mother has been forgetting things lately, and she saw her mom almost overdose on her medications because she forgot she had already taken them. When she stopped her from taking them, Janice got very upset with her. She's worried that Janice may harm herself if something isn't done.

Case Study I

Henry, a 67 yo M, presents to your clinic with his wife. She tells you that Henry has been having problems walking lately. He seems to have lost his balance, and his walk is more of a "shuffle" now. She also tells you she noticed that when he talks, he sounds like he's muffling his words.
[]

Case Study J

Velma, a 30 yo F, presents herself to your clinic with her husband. She tells you that she is tired all the time and has problems walking. She tells you that one day she'll wake up and her right arm won't move, and the next day her left leg won't move. She also tells you her vision seems to be getting worse all the time.

Case Study K

Roger, a 35 yo M, presents himself to your clinic and tells you that he has been having problems chewing and swallowing his food. He also tells you every once in a while his muscles will contract on their own, and he twitches involuntarily.

Case Study L

Bill, a 73 yo M, presents himself to your clinic with his wife. She tells you that at dinner the other night, Bill started having a seizure, which has never happened before. He tells you he has been having headaches and has been starting to have some memory issues.
