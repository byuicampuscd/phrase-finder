ECEN 150 Laboratory Experiment: Circuit Proto-Typing

Purpose:

1.  Practice laying out components and wires on a blank, perforated board. 

2.  Practice soldering (and perhaps, desoldering).

3.  Be able it identify and explain the basic operation of some common electronic components.

4.  Have some fun!

 

Equipment & Materials:

*** Safety Glasses ***

Soldering Iron

Desoldering Tools (pump or solder braid)

Needle Nose Pliers

Diagonal Cutters

Wire Stripper

Parts Kit

Solder

9V Battery

Procedure:

[

Build this simple oscillator circuit and make it work.  (It should work like this.)

]

Notes:

1.  The resistors and capacitors in your parts kit may have slightly different values than those shown in the schematic diagram.

2.  Follow these links for more information about identifying the components and connecting them correctly:

Resistors -- You need 5 of these.  Make sure you can tell them apart!

Capacitors -- They are polarized!  That means you must connect them properly or they will explode like this!

LEDs -- They will not explode (like polarized capacitors) if you put them in wrong, but they will not work backwards.  These are heat sensitive!

Transistors -- Like diodes, they will not work if you get the pins mixed up.  Follow the pin diagram for the TO92B case on this link for your 2N3904 NPN transistors.  These are heat sensitive!

3.  The red wire on the battery clip goes to the +9V connection on the diagram.

4.  The black wire on the battery clip goes to the "ground"    on the diagram. 

5.  Because there are two grounds, you must connect them together with a piece of insulated wire.  Always use insulated wires where they cross each other to prevent "short" circuits.

6.  Photographs of a working circuit can be seen here.

6.  There is no formal lab report for this lab exercise!  Simply make it work and present it for grading.  You will be scored on the quality of your solder connections, and whether both LEDs actually blink.
