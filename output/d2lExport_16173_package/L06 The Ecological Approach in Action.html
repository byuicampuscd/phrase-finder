The Ecological Approach in Action

Sometimes it can be difficult wrap your head around what a given theory of model is really saying. If you are still scratching your head about Bronfenbrenner's model, you are probably not alone!

At the bottom of the page is a 2012 presentation given at BYU-Idaho, in which Kelli Houghton (board member from United Families International) discusses the lessons she has learned in her family advocacy experience. Note how she uses the ecological approach in her own advocacy. This will likely not only be inspiring and a bit shocking, but should also give you a better understanding of Ecological Systems Theory.

"All that is necessary for the triumph of evil is that good men [and women] do nothing."

In 2012, Kelli Houghton, MSW, and board member of United Families International came to the BYU-Idaho campus and spoke on what she has learned about child and family advocacy. Please watch attentively and take notes of the points that strike you the most.

  • How did Kelli's presentation help your understanding of the ecological approach to family advocacy?
  • What system or level do you personally feel would be the most difficult to advocate? Why?
  • What system or level do you personally feel is the most important to advocate? Why?
  • What historical events and factors (chronosystem) can you think of that have contributed to the disintigration of the family?
  • What questions do you still have?
