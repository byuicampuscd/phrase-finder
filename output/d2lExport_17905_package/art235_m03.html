Read | Logo Project

A logo is a visual symbol that represents an entity such as a business. In this project you will be designing logos that combines a word (noun) and a service business. Do not include words. The logo should clearly communicate the word and the type of service. The logos will be market tested to determine what they communicate to a first time viewer. This project is about your ability to create a well designed logo that clearly communicate what it represents.

Objectives

  • Focus on combining an object and a service as part of the creative process.
  • Generate three logos that combine two words into believable companies.
  • User test the logos to assess the communication.
  • Develop beautiful logos.

Concept Development | thumbnails Sketches: three sets (45 points)

Explore a wide range of visual possibilities through thumbnail sketching. Focus on combining and communicating the object and a service business. Generate three two words sets (noun + service) into believable companies and make a minimum of 30 sketches for each set.

Sketching is visual brainstorming. Don't edit yourself at this point. Sketch out your concepts, letting one idea inform the next. Keep going until you generate 30 unique ideas. The sketches should be quick. If necessary use a timer and spend about one minute per sketch.

The sketches should be quick. If necessary use a timer and spend about one minute per sketch. Upon completing the sketches mark 3–5 of the most promising concepts.

Assessment: (45 points | 15 points each) The student can use thumbnail sketching to explore a wide range of logo concepts. The sketches focus on concept & form with an emphasis on concept. (Each of the three sets will be evaluated individually)

  • Are there a minimum of 30 sketches?
  • Do the sketches explore ideas more than style?
  • Are the sketches clear and understandable?
  • Are three to five sketches identified as possibilities?
  • Is typographic hierarchy apparent and used successfully on the page?

Digital sketches (15 points)

Choose three different directions from each word set and create digital sketches to explore form. The focus is still on concept & form but this time form should be emphasized. The logos should only use black and white.

Assessment: (15 points) The student can use a vector drawing program to sketch and refine logo concept ideas. The student should be able to recognize the difference between a logo that is communicating and a logo that is just stylistic.
