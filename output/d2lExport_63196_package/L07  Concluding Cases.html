L08 Concluding Cases

Due by End of Week, see Calendar

Estimated Time: 2 hours

Instructions

Use the Concluding Cases to connect the disease information you studied to additional real-world situations.

Step 01

Follow the case study process to critically evaluate each situation described below. You can complete this activity on your own before reviewing with your study partner or groups, or you may complete this activity for the first time with your study partner or group.

[]

Case Study A

Chris, a 15 yo M, presents himself to your clinic and tells you that he has recently gone through a “growth spurt.” He is 6’10’’ tall. Chris says that his hands, feet, and tongue have grown. His skin has become very oily, and he has been having headaches for the past few months.

Case Study B

Wilma, a 6 yo F, is brought to your clinic by her mother. Her mother is concerned because Wilma isn’t growing like the other children her age. She is often tired and complains that she’s cold all the time. She also tells you she notices that she seems frail and weak.

Case Study C

Marvin, a 36 yo M, presents himself to your clinic and tells you for the past few weeks he has been urinating about every 5 minutes. He says he is constantly thirsty and has noticed that his skin is extremely dry, even when he puts lotion on.
[]

Case Study D

Aboyo is a 48 yo F from Nigeria. She presents herself to your clinic and tells you she has noticed a lump forming on the front of her neck. She said it started out small, but it has gradually gotten larger over time.

Case Study E

Agnes, a 40 yo F, presents herself to your clinic and tells you she’s been having trouble sleeping. As you talk to her you notice her eyeballs are protruding. She tells you she sweats a lot, which is not usually a problem with her. She shows you patches on her legs that appear to have an orange color.

Case Study F

Harriett, a 43 yo F, presents herself to your clinic and tells you she has been experiencing very dry skin although she religiously applies moisturizer to her skin throughout the day. She says she has a lump on her neck and it has been painful and tender. She also tells you she has been extremely tired, intolerant to cold weather, and depressed.
[]

Case Study G

Ben, a 26 yo M, presents himself to your clinic and tells you he has had constipation for a while and has been extremely tired. He tells you his muscles keep cramping up and his thinking is clouded sometimes. His skin appears dry upon physical examination.

Case Study H

Stella, a 36 yo F, presents herself to your clinic and tells you she has noticed a lump in her neck and it is difficult to swallow. She tells you she thought she had a cold because she was losing her voice, but that started a few months ago.

Case Study I

Bruce, a 44 yo M, presents himself to your clinic and tells you he has been experiencing joint pain. He is frequently urinating and has been vomiting. He is always tired and he has been forgetting things lately. Bruce also reports multiple bouts of kidney stones.

Case Study J

Sandy, a 35 yo F, presents herself to your clinic and tells you she has been experiencing a tingling and painful sensation in her hands and feet. She is also experiencing problems remembering things. She has been having painful menstrual cycles, is losing her hair, and her finger nails have become more brittle.
