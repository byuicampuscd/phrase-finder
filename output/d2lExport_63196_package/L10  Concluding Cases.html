L11 Concluding Cases

Due by End of Week, see Calendar

Estimated Time: 2 hours

Instructions

Use the Concluding Cases to connect the disease information you studied to additional real-world situations.

Step 01

Follow the case study process to critically evaluate each situation described below. You can complete this activity on your own before reviewing with your study partner or groups, or you may complete this activity for the first time with your study partner or group.

[]

Case Study A

Charlie, a 22 yo M, presents himself to your clinic with blisters on his lips. He tells you it is hard to eat and swallow. You ask him when he noticed these blisters and he tells you he went on a date last weekend with his girlfriend, who had a similar blister, and his appeared a few days later. You ask if he by chance kissed her or shared chapstick with her, and he sheepishly replies that he may have kissed her once or twice.

Case Study B

Gloria, a 25 yo F, presents herself to your clinic and tells you she has been experiencing heartburn and nausea. You ask her if she notices an increase in this heartburn after eating spicy foods, and she says no, but it does increase when she lays down to go to bed.

Case Study C

Doug, a 30 yo M, presents to your clinic and tells you he has been throwing up blood. He has stomach cramps and indigestion. He says he has been taking Tums (an over-the-counter antacid), but nothing helps.
[]

Case Study D

Yolanda, a 36 yo F, presents herself to your clinic and tells you she has been experiencing heartburn and indigestion and a pain in her stomach that won’t go away. She also describes a sensation she gets in the back of her throat that feels like there’s boiling water in the back of her throat.

Case Study E

Vernon, a 30 yo M, presents himself to your clinic and tells you he has been experiencing diarrhea and vomiting for the past several days. He says his stomach is so upset that he can hear a rumbling noise in his stomach.You ask him if he is allergic to any foods and he tells you he has had an allergic reaction to seafood before, but he usually just gets a rash when he eats it, so it doesn’t stop him from going to Red Lobster (a seafood restaurant) every Saturday.

Case Study F

Neil, a 56 yo M, presents himself to your clinic and tells you he has been constipated for several days. He explains that it is very painful for him to try to have a bowel movement, and he has cramps in his lower abdomen.
[]

Case Study G

Fred, a 19 yo M, presents himself to your clinic and tells you he is having severe pain in the upper right side of his body, but now the pain has moved to the lower right side. He has been vomiting and has been constipated for the past week.

Case Study H

Kristen, a 28 yo F, presents herself to your clinic and says that when she goes to the bathroom, there is blood in her rectum. She says it is uncomfortable to sit. You ask if Kristen has had any children, and she tells you she just had a baby two months ago.

Case Study I

Will, a 7 yo M, is brought to your clinic by his mother. She says that Will has been experiencing extremely loose stools and stomach cramping. She thinks he has a fever and he doesn’t show any interest in eating.
[]

Case Study J

Vickie, a 32 yo F, presents herself to your clinic and tells you she has heartburn and severe pain in her stomach. You ask her where the pain is and she points right above the diaphragm. She says this has been going on for the past few weeks and the pain is getting to be unbearable.

Case Study K

Carlos, a 40 yo M, presents himself to your clinic and tells you he has been experiencing a sharp pain in his stomach. Carlos works at a grocery store and lifts heavy boxes all day. He said the pain is severe when he lifts the boxes, but bearable at other times. He tells you he has also been constipated for several days and he wonders if that’s why he is experiencing this pain.

Case Study L

Eric, a 25 yo M, presents himself to your clinic with severe pain in the upper right half of his body and tells you the pain is going through his body into his back. He tells you that he went to McDonald’s and got a Big Mac (large fast-food hamburger) and immediately afterwards felt extremely sick and vomited. He says he has really bad heartburn and has episodes of flatulence as well.
[]

Case Study M

Paul, a 46 yo M, presents himself to your clinic and tells you that he has been vomiting and has no desire to eat. His legs are starting to swell and he has noticed his skin is turning a yellowish color. You ask Paul if he drinks, and he informs you that he’s been drinking since he was a sophomore in high school.

Case Study N

Tracy, a 43 yo F, presents herself to your clinic and says she has been experiencing flu-like symptoms for the past several weeks. She is always tired and has no desire to eat. She says she has noticed her stools are a weird color, almost the color of a clay pot, and her urine is dark. She has also noticed that her skin is turning yellow.

Case Study O

Oscar, a 40 yo M, presents himself to your clinic and tells you he found blood in his stools and his stomach is swollen. You perform an examination and find that his liver is enlarged. You ask about his diet and he tells you he eats a lot of beef because he’s from Argentina and that’s what their diet mainly consisted of when he was growing up.

Case Study P

Rita, a 48 yo F, presents herself to your clinic and tells you she is having pain in her stomach that radiates to her back and her skin is turning yellow. She tells you she is tired most of the time and has started having diarrhea. You ask if she drinks and she tells you no, but she does smoke a pack of cigarettes a day. You ask about her diet and she tells you she usually goes to fast-food restaurants because she’s so busy she doesn’t have time to cook.
