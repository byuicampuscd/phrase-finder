Rose Marie Murray

United Families Utah President

In 2015, Utah State Legislators passed a non-discrimination ordinance that appealed to both the LGBTQ and religious communities. It is the first of its kind and has been named a model state non-discrimination policy for other states. The sponsor for this bill before it was passed mentioned he felt God's hand in the creation of such an ordinance just a few months before the Supreme Court ruling on marriage. Just after this ordinance passed, the FAML 460 Course had an Advocacy field trip with Tim Rarick and met with Senator Adams who lead the ordinance. Senator Adams said something that really made a lot of sense. He said that it was miraculous that the ordinance passed just prior to the Supreme Court ruling on same-sex marriage and he didn't think they would have gotten so many religious freedom clauses approved after the ruling. He said that, truly, we cannot discriminate when it comes to housing and employment. These are basic rights for all! Imagine if a same-sex companionship came to an apartment complex and the manager denied them housing and the couple took that complex to court. They would win! Having a place to live is a basic human right. But by creating an ordinance that protected these rights, they were able to protect religious freedom as well. 
Keep that in mind as you look over this pdf.
