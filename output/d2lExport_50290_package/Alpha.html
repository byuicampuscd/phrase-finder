Alpha Particle

View Image
Examples
Americium-241
Uranium-238
Plutonium-236
Thorium-232
Radium-226
Radon-222
Polonium-210
Nature of Radiation

Made up of two protons and two neutrons.

It is similar to a Helium atom missing its electron.

How Created
When the ratio of neutrons to protons in the nucleus is too low, certain atoms restore the balance by emitting alpha particles from the decaying nucleus.
How far it travels
It can travel a few centimeters through the air before gaining 2 electrons to become a neutral helium atom.
Stopped by
Outer layer of skin, clothing or paper.

Beta Particle

View Image
Examples
Tritium
Calcium-46
Iron-59
Cobalt-60
Strontium-90
Technetium-99
Iodine 129, 131
Cesium-137
Nature of Radiation
Is identical to an electron but is ejected from the nucleus rather than an electron shell.
How Created
When the ratio of neutrons to protons in the nucleus is too high. An excess neutron transforms into a proton and an electron. The proton stays in the nucleus and the electron is ejected from the decaying nucleus.
How far it travels
It can travel 1-3 meters through the air.
Stopped by
25cm of wood
1 cm of aluminum
0.5cm of body tissue

Gamma Rays

Examples
Beta Emitters
Chromium-51
Arsenic-74
Technetium-99
Nature of Radiation
Small wave lengths of energy, Photons originating from the nucleus.
How Created
When the beta particle ejection doesn't rid the nucleus of the extra energy, the nucleus releases the remaining excess energy in the form of a gamma photon.
How far it travels
100's of meters through the air.
Stopped by
3 centimeters of lead or several feet of concrete
