L03 Concluding Cases

Due by End of Week, see Calendar

Estimated Time: 2 hours

Instructions

Use the Concluding Cases to connect the disease information you studied to real-world situations.

Step 01

Follow the case study process to critically evaluate each situation described below. You can complete this activity on your own before reviewing with your study partner or groups, or you may complete this activity for the first time with your study partner or group.

[]

Case Study A

Troy, a 9-month-old infant, is brought to your clinic by his mother. She tells you that she has noticed that Troy doesn’t try to crawl, but mainly uses his hands to pull himself around. She finds this unusual because her other children had started crawling by this age. You question the mother about her health during pregnancy and she admits that she had diabetes during the pregnancy.

Case Study B

Dylan and David, twin infants, are brought to your clinic by their mother. The mother tells you that both boys are having difficulty eating and when she feeds them the formula comes out their noses. Upon physical examination, you find that Dylan has a cleft in his upper lip and David has a hole in the top of his mouth.

Case Study C

Cynthia, a 2-month-old infant, is brought to your clinic by her mother. She tells you that Cynthia has been having trouble eating. As you examine Cynthia, you first notice her skin has a blueish hue and frothy white bubbles in her mouth. You order an X-ray to be done and find that Cynthia’s esophagus has not formed completely.
[]

Case Study D

A mother brings her new baby, Max, into your clinic and tells you that he has been projectile vomiting since he was born. Upon physical examination, you find a mass in Max’s epigastric region.

Case Study E

A 2-month-old baby, Grace, is brought to the clinic by her father. Grace is pulling her legs up and crying as you examine her. Her father says she has had bloody stools and has recently started to throw up. As you continue your examination, you find that Grace’s small stomach appears to be bloated.

Case Study F

Alex, a 2-day-old infant, is brought to your clinic by her mother. She is worried because Alex still hasn’t had a bowel movement. As you examine Alex, you find that his abdomen is swollen. Upon further examination it also appears Alex has some evidence of Down Syndrome.
[]

Case Study G

Tanner, a 1 yo M, is brought to the clinic by his mother. She tells you she has noticed something odd when she changes Tanner’s diapers. Tanner appears to only have one testicle and she is concerned that he has a birth defect that will affect him later in life.

Case Study H

An infant, Julie, is brought to your clinic by her mother. Her mother tells you that Julie didn’t have a bowel movement during the first day after she was born and the doctors were worried. Since then, Julie has developed a productive cough and has not gained weight. Her mom also tells you that when she kisses her baby, Julie’s skin tastes salty.

Case Study I

Alisha, a two-month-old infant, was born with three copies of the 21st chromosome instead of two. She has a flat nose and a tongue that is larger than normal. Alisha also has white spots on the colored part of her eyes.
