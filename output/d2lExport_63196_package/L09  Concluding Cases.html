L10 Concluding Cases

Due by End of Week, see Calendar

Estimated Time: 2 hours

Instructions

Use the Concluding Cases to connect the disease information you studied to additional real-world situations.

Step 01

Follow the case study process to critically evaluate each situation described below. You can complete this activity on your own before reviewing with your study partner or groups, or you may complete this activity for the first time with your study partner or group.

[]

Case Study A

Mindy, a 20 yo F, presents herself to your clinic and tells you she has been experiencing a sore throat and chills. You examine her throat and neck and find that her lymph glands are swollen. She says her boyfriend had the same thing a few weeks ago. You ask if she has shared a drink or something else with her boyfriend since this time and she says she has had a sip of his soda a few times.

Case Study B

Bryce, a 12 yo M, is brought to your clinic by his mother. Bryce has been coughing up “phlegm” and has been having pain in his chest. His mother says he has a fever but is always shaking and complaining he is cold.

Case Study C

Leah, a 21 yo F, presents herself to your clinic complaining of a headache and nausea. She has a high fever and has been coughing up a grayish sputum. She tells you this all started after she went hot tubbing with some friends at a local hotel.
[]

Case Study D

Kevin, a 71 yo M, presents to your clinic and tells you he has had a chronic cough for “years.” He has smoked one pack of cigarettes per day for the last 55 years. On physical exam you notice that his chest has an uncharacteristic barrel shape to it.

Case Study E

Holly, a 60 yo F, presents herself to your clinic and complains of a “constant cough.” She has been coughing up sputum and is struggling to breathe. On examination you hear Holly wheezing when she breathes. She also tells you she has noticed a bluish hue to her skin.

Case Study F

Jordan, a 23 yo M, presents to your clinic and tells you he has been experiencing tightness in his chest and shortness of breath. He says that lately he has been coughing a lot, and it usually gets worse when he goes to bed. He said that he recalls his mother having the same problem when he was living with his parents before his mission.
[]

Case Study G

Carol, a 63 yo F, presents herself to your clinic with her husband. Carol was diagnosed with pneumonia, but now she is coughing up a bloody substance. She is having chest pain and has episodes of sweating and chills and a fever. She also tells you that she can’t catch her breath sometimes.

Case Study H

Harold, a 25 yo M, presents himself to your clinic and tells you he has had a cough and has a lack of energy. At night he will wake up drenched in sweat and experiences chest pain and wheezing when he breathes. He tells you he recently got back from a vacation in Mexico and the symptoms appeared after that.

Case Study I

A distraught mother brings her 10 week old infant, Jean, into the emergency department. Jean is not breathing and has mottled skin. Her mother says that she laid Jean down for a nap and when she came in to check on her, there was blood on the blanket by her head and she wasn’t breathing. You try to revive Jean, but your efforts are useless.
[]

Case Study J

Eddie, a 10 yo M, is brought to the clinic by his mother. His mom tells you that at night she can hear Eddie snoring even though his room isn’t close to her room. She also tells you he is constantly having head colds and she’s worried that something might be wrong with his sinuses.

Case Study K

Susie, a 6 week-old infant, is brought to your clinic by her mother. Her mother says that she noticed white, velvety lesions in Susie’s mouth and on her tongue. She noticed that her tongue would start bleeding when she started to feed Susie.

Case Study L

Luke, a 9 mo F, is brought to your clinic by his mother. Luke has a fever and bark-like cough. You listen to Luke’s breathing, and you hear a wheezing noise whenever he breathes in air.
