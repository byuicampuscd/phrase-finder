Case Study: Protein Energy Malnutrition

Estimated time to complete: 90 minutes.

Protein energy malnutrition (PEM) is a term that relates to a group of related conditions including kwashiorkor and marasmus. Famine, political unrest, and civil war contribute to the PEM found in many poor countries throughout the world. Children are often the age group hardest hit.

The LDS Humanitarian and Catholic Services have joined forces to help in the efforts to relieve malnutrition in developing nations such as Ethiopia.

Activity

The purpose of this activity is to increase your awareness of the impact of protein energy malnutrition on children and introduce you to one of the efforts of the LDS Humanitarian program to help improve the conditions.
