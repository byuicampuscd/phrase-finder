L06 Concluding Cases

Due by End of Week, see Calendar

Estimated Time: 2 hours

Instructions

Use the Concluding Cases to connect the disease information you studied to additional real-world situations.

Step 01

Follow the case study process to critically evaluate each situation described below. You can complete this activity on your own before reviewing with your study partner or groups, or you may complete this activity for the first time with your study partner or group.

[]

Case Study A

Martha, a 60 yo F, presents herself to your clinic and tells you she has been experiencing pain in her hips and lower back. She tells you that she fell last year and broke her hip, and she’s experienced pain ever since.

Case Study B

Stella, a 60 yo F, presents herself to your clinic and tells you that she fell a few months ago and broke her hip, and she has been having severe pain in that hip ever since. She tells you she has had lower back pain for a few years as well, but isn’t sure if that has anything to do with the pain in her hip.

Case Study C

William, a 10 yo M, presents himself to your clinic with his parents. They tell you William has been in extreme pain due to severe swelling in his leg. You examine the leg and find it to be swollen and red, and tender to the touch. You ask the parents if William has had any sort of infections recently. They tell you that William fell and injured that leg a while ago and he got a Staph infection.
[]

Case Study D

Ken, a 46 yo M, presents himself to your clinic and tells you he has had a constant pain in his lower leg for several weeks and has recently started having terrible headaches. He explains that he seems to be losing his hearing and believes it is because of the severity of his headaches.

Case Study E

Miriam, a 6 yo F, is brought to your clinic by her mother. Her mother tells you Miriam fell off of her bike and started screaming that her arm hurt. You examine her arm and find it to be swollen and discolored. You ask Miriam if she can move her arm, and she tells you she can’t because it hurts too much.

Case Study F

Calvin, a 16 yo M, presents himself to your clinic and tells you he was playing basketball and “jammed” his thumb, but the pain is extremely intense. You examine his thumb and find that it has a deformed appearance to it.
[]

Case Study G

Joan, a 55 yo F, presents herself to your clinic and tells you her knees ache all the time, and the only time she doesn’t feel pain is when she’s sleeping. She tells you her knees are always stiff and sore in the morning and it takes her a while to get moving when she gets up.

Case Study H

Larry, a 60 yo M, presents himself to your clinic and tells you he has been experiencing stiffness in his fingers and wrists. He tells you it is sometimes too painful to even hold a pen, and he has noticed the more he uses his hands, the worse the pain gets. 

Case Study I

Kevin, a 46 yo M, presents himself to your clinic along with his wife. His wife tells you that last night Kevin woke up screaming that his big toe hurt, even though she hadn’t touched him. He tells you that he’s never experienced something like this before, but he recalls his dad having something similar, although he doesn’t remember what the diagnosis was.
[]

Case Study J

Melanie, a 17 yo F, presents herself to your clinic and tells you she was playing soccer when all of a sudden her foot twisted wrong and intense pain shot through her ankle. You examine the ankle and find it to be black and blue. You ask her to move her foot in a circular motion, but she is unable to do so.

Case Study K

Stan, a 26 yo M, presents himself to your clinic and tells you he has been experiencing extreme pain in his elbow. He tells you he is an avid tennis player and the pain is starting to affect his game. He tells you that after a while he can’t even move his arm because it gets stiff and sore.

Case Study L

Candace, a 50 yo F, presents herself to your clinic and tells you she has had pain and a burning sensation in her wrists for the past few weeks. You ask her to ball her hand up into a fist, and she is unable to do so. You ask her what she does for a living and she tells you she works at a doctor’s office and spends her day typing up reports and updating patient records.

Case Study M

Drew is a 21 yo M who presents himself to your clinic and tells you he has been experiencing pain in his feet. He tells you he works at a grocery store as a clerk and stands for a lot of the day and when he gets home the pain is almost unbearable.
